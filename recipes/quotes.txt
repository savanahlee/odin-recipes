<p><a href="https://thecozycook.com/swedish-meatball-recipe/" class="custom-link no-lightbox" title="Swedish-Meatballs-1" aria-label="" onclick="event.stopPropagation()" target="_self" rel=""><img decoding="async" class="aligncenter wp-image-46512 size-full" src="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Swedish-Meatballs-1.jpg" alt="Swedish Meatballs in a skillet with a gravy cream sauce and fresh parsley." width="850" height="1207" data-pin-title="Swedish Meatballs" data-pin-description="These Swedish Meatballs are easy to make and have the most flavorful gravy cream sauce! " srcset="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Swedish-Meatballs-1.jpg 850w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Swedish-Meatballs-1-500x710.jpg 500w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Swedish-Meatballs-1-700x994.jpg 700w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Swedish-Meatballs-1-768x1091.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 850px) 100vw, 850px" /></a></p>
<h2>Swedish Meatballs</h2>
<p>I&#8217;ll just go ahead and say it: I do believe that these Swedish Meatballs are quite possibly the <strong><em>best of the best!</em></strong></p>
<p>These are not an IKEA replica, (<em>I&#8217;ve actually never been to IKEA! </em>🤯) and I can&#8217;t say that I&#8217;ve been to Sweden either, but they <em>are&nbsp;</em>amazing, and include some standout ingredients that we&#8217;ve come to know and love in America when it comes to this particular dish.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The meatballs themselves are made with a combination of <strong>ground beef and pork,</strong> along with a <em>touch</em> of <strong>allspice</strong> and <strong>nutmeg</strong>. <strong>Onions</strong> and <strong>garlic</strong> add flavor and texture, and <strong>milk, egg,</strong> and <strong>breadcrumbs</strong> act as a binder. Finally, <strong>Parmesan cheese is</strong> a little &#8220;Cozy&#8221; touch that adds a subtle pop of flavor.</p>
<p>The sauce itself is an amazing combination of <strong>beef broth,</strong> <strong>Worcestershire sauce, Dijon, and sour cream</strong>. My secret ingredient? <strong><em>Chicken Bouillon.&nbsp;</em></strong>It adds the best depth of flavor and contrasts perfectly with the beef broth.</p>
<h2>How to Make It</h2>
<p><span style="color: #000080;"><em>See recipe card below this post for ingredient quantities and full instructions.</em></span></p>
<p><strong>Combine the breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, egg, milk, onions, garlic, salt, oregano, allspice, nutmeg, and pepper. Gently incorporate the meat until evenly combined.</strong></p>
<p><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-46471 size-full" src="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-4.jpg" alt="Mixing ground beef, pork, and meatball ingredients in a glass bowl to make Swedish meatballs." width="1000" height="600" data-pin-nopin="true" srcset="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-4.jpg 1000w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-4-500x300.jpg 500w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-4-700x420.jpg 700w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-4-768x461.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<p><strong>Roll into 1+ 1/2 inch meatballs and chill for 15 minutes, (or overnight). Brown in batches and set aside.</strong></p>
<p><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-46470 size-full" src="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-3.jpg" alt="Swedish meatballs on a tray before and after cooking." width="1000" height="600" data-pin-nopin="true" srcset="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-3.jpg 1000w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-3-500x300.jpg 500w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-3-700x420.jpg 700w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-3-768x461.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<p><strong>Melt butter in the same skillet and whisk in flour. Add the sauce mixture </strong><em>(beef broth, chicken bouillon, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon, and parsley)</em><strong>, in small splashes, stirring continuously. Add the sour cream to a medium bowl along with some of the sauce, and stir until combined. Stir it into the skillet.</strong></p>
<p><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-46468 size-full" src="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-1.jpg" alt="Making Swedish meatball sauce in a skillet with roux, beef broth, and sour cream." width="1000" height="600" data-pin-nopin="true" srcset="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-1.jpg 1000w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-1-500x300.jpg 500w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-1-700x420.jpg 700w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-1-768x461.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<p><strong>Add the meatballs back and spoon the sauce on top. Cover partially, and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until cooked through. Serve over <a href="https://thecozycook.com/homemade-mashed-potatoes/">mashed potatoes</a> or <a href="https://thecozycook.com/buttered-noodles/">egg noodles</a>!</strong></p>
<p><img decoding="async" loading="lazy" class="aligncenter wp-image-46469 size-full" src="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-2.jpg" alt="Adding Swedish meatballs to a skillet and spooning sauce on top." width="1000" height="600" data-pin-nopin="true" srcset="https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-2.jpg 1000w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-2-500x300.jpg 500w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-2-700x420.jpg 700w, https://thecozycook.com/wp-content/uploads/1984/07/Swedish-Meatballs-2-768x461.jpg 768w" sizes="(max-width: 1000px) 100vw, 1000px" /></p>
<h2>Using Frozen Meatballs</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Frozen meatballs can be used in a pinch</strong>, just note that the <strong>allspice</strong> and <strong>nutmeg</strong> seasonings are what give the meatballs themselves that traditional Swedish meatball flavor, consider adding 1/4 tsp. of each to the sauce itself instead.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Make Ahead Method</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Roll out the meatballs, cover, and refrigerate for up to 2 days</strong> prior to serving, then follow recipe as outlined.</li>
<li>You can also<strong>&nbsp;flash freeze the uncooked meatballs on a tray for 1 hour. Transfer them to a freezer bag and freeze for up to 3 months.</strong> Thaw in the refrigerator overnight, then follow recipe as outlined.</li>
</ul>
<blockquote class="tips">
<h2>Pro Tips</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>The chicken bouillon in the sauce adds depth of flavor</strong> and contrasts nicely with the beef broth. Beef bouillon can be used instead if needed.</li>
<li><strong>Use full-fat sour cream</strong> to ensure that it doesn’t curdle.</li>
<li><strong>Heavy cream</strong> <strong>can be used instead of sour cream</strong> if preferred.</li>
<li><strong>Any combination of ground beef, pork, and veal</strong> can be used in this recipe.</li>
<li><strong>1 + 1/4 lbs. of meat</strong> is perfect for fitting in a 12-inch, high-walled skillet.</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<h2>Storage</h2>
<ul>
<li>Store in an airtight container and <strong>refrigerate for up to 3 days</strong> or<strong> freeze for up to 3 months.</strong></li>
<li>Leftovers freeze fairly well, let them thaw prior to reheating.</li>
<li>Reheat in a <a href="https://www.bonappetit.com/story/double-boiler" target="_blank" rel="noopener">makeshift double boiler</a> for best results. Refrain from reheating over heat that is too high, as the sauce can break.</li>
</ul>

	<div class="wprm-recipe-notes-container wprm-block-text-normal"><h3 class="wprm-recipe-header wprm-recipe-notes-header wprm-block-text-normal wprm-align-left wprm-header-decoration-none" style="">Notes</h3><div class="wprm-recipe-notes"><span style="display: block;"><strong>Pro Tips:</strong></span><div class="wprm-spacer"></div>
<ul>
<li><strong>The chicken bouillon in the sauce adds depth of flavor</strong> and contrasts nicely with the beef broth. Beef bouillon can be used instead if needed.</li>
<li><strong>Use full-fat sour cream</strong> to ensure that it doesn’t curdle.</li>
<li><strong>Heavy cream</strong> <strong>can be used instead of sour cream</strong> if preferred.</li>
<li><strong>Any combination of ground beef, pork, and veal</strong> can be used in this recipe.</li>
<li><strong>1 + 1/4 lbs. of meat</strong> is perfect for fitting in a 12-inch, high-walled skillet.</li>
</ul>

<span style="display: block;"><strong>Storage:</strong></span><div class="wprm-spacer"></div>
<ul>
<li>Store in an airtight container and <strong>refrigerate for up to 3 days</strong> or<strong> freeze for up to 3 months.</strong></li>
<li>Leftovers freeze fairly well, let them thaw prior to reheating.</li>
<li>Reheat in a <a href="https://www.bonappetit.com/story/double-boiler" target="_blank" rel="noopener nofollow sponsored">makeshift double boiler</a> for best results. Refrain from reheating over heat that is too high, as the sauce can break.</li>
</ul>
