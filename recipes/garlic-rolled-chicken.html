Garlic and Butter Rolled Chichen





Description

Hot out of the oven finger food, these cute little chicken bites are savory and addictable. Don't make too many or your audience will fill up before the meal. With corn flakes to crush and demolish, it can be a fun activity for little hands to help or for adults who want to get their angst out. It cooks fast and, prepared ahead of time, they can be popped in the oven and ready by the time you finish a round of who wants what to drink. This fun finger food is protien packed to serve a family running by the kitchen looking to have a snack thats going to last on their way to work or play and eventually to the next meal.

Some slicing and dicing is the main work here. You can make it easier by buying chichen already cut into thin tenders and a jar of diced garlic or you can pound out a chicken breast and be picky picky about trimming out all grizzle and fat, with fresh chopped garlic. Either way, this is over the top with a sweet and savory flavor.



Ingredients

  • A stick or two of softened (not melted) butter
  • As much chopped garlic as you want
  • Any herbs will fit into this recipe, but some dashes of Italian seasoning, salt and pepper work well
  • A box of corn flakes crushed into oblivion
  • A box of toothpicks
  • Cooking spray


Steps

  1. Not necessary, but take the time to pound out the chicken and it makes for an easier rolling process after slicing into thin strips. No worries about some shorter or longer than others.
  2. Combine the softened butter with the garlic and any herbs if desired. Here's a chance to be interestingly creative or keep it deliciously simple with your seasoned butter.
  3. Spread the butter on one side of the chicken strips and, witout pressing out the butter, roll into balls
  4. take each rolled piece and roll them into the demolished corn flakes, the more you can press a bit on there the better.
  5. Place a toothpick into each roll to keep into place while cooking and for ease of grabbing when the crowd comes running with the aroma
  6. Bake at 350ish for about 20 minutes. Let cool for but a few minutes, and let them at it.
